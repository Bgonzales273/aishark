About Sharks ðŸ¦ˆ

Sharks are a group of elasmobranch fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, five to seven gill slits on the sides of the head, and pectoral fins that are not fused to the head.

Great White Shark ðŸ¦ˆ

The Great White Shark is known for its size, with larger female individuals growing to 6.1 m (20 ft) in length and 1,905 kg (4,200 lb) in weight.

Tiger Shark ðŸ¦ˆ

The Tiger Shark is a solitary, mostly nocturnal hunter. It is notable for its stripes which fade as the shark matures.

Hammerhead Shark ðŸ¦ˆ

Hammerhead Sharks are easily recognizable by their distinctive head shape, which is flattened and laterally extended into a "hammer" shape.

Shark Habitat ðŸ¦ˆ

Sharks are found in all oceans and are common to depths of 2,000 meters (6,600 ft). They generally do not live in freshwater, with the exception of the bull shark and river shark.

Shark Conservation ðŸ¦ˆ

Many shark species are threatened by human activities. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these magnificent creatures and maintain the balance of marine ecosystems.
